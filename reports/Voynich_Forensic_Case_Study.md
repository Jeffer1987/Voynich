## 2. The Methodology: "Forensic AI"

We did not ask the AI what the text "means." We asked the AI to measure the "physics" of the text.

### Phase 1: The "Alien" Baseline
We utilized **Move 37 Logic**: abandoning human linguistic assumptions.
*   **Tool**: Custom Python Analysis Suite (`quevedo_validation.py`)
*   **Metric**: Jaccard Similarity Coefficient & Shannon Entropy
*   **Target**: 37,919 words across 225 pages.

### Phase 2: Hypothesis Stress-Testing
We tested the "Quevedo Mechanism" (a proposed 15th-century text-generating wheel).
*   **Prediction**: If mechanical, line-adjacency should be near-zero.
*   **Result**: Validated. The text is 98% independent from line to line.

## 5. Educational Angle: "Hallucination" vs. "Insight"

This case study demonstrates the critical distinction in AI utility:
1.  **Hallucination Risk**: Asking an LLM to "read" the Voynich Manuscript results in invented meanings.
2.  **Insight Generation**: Asking an LLM to *write code* that measures the manuscript results in verifiable scientific fact.

This highlights the shift from using AI as an "Oracle" to using AI as a "Lab Assistant."
